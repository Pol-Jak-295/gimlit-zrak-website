What is air quality?

Air quality is the degree to which air is suitable or clean enough for humans, animals, and plants. The air quality is determined by the amount of pollutants in the air. The most common pollutants are particulate matter, nitrogen dioxide, sulfur dioxide, carbon monoxide, and ozone. The air quality can be affected by natural sources, such as volcanoes and wildfires, and human activities, such as transportation, industry, and agriculture.

Most common pollutants are:

  1. PM-10, PM-2.5 and BC (black carbon)
    • The number after pm is the size of particles (10, 2.5 nanometers), black carbon however is a lot smaller, and is therefore able to enter cells of the body
  2. Gasseous pollutants
    • Those pollutants fall into the category of gasses, so they are inhaled into the lungs of a living being
    • such pollutants are:
      • NO2
      • SO2
      • CO
      • O3
